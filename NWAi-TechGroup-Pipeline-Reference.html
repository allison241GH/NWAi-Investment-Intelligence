The Bigger Picture — Six Groups, One Platform

TechGroup is the first live instantiation. The architecture is designed to scale across all six NWAi investment groups.

Shared rails. Group-specific playbooks.
Think of this platform like a franchise model: the kitchen equipment and workflow are identical in every location — the same pipeline stages, the same AI agents, the same Dealum CRM, the same output formats. What changes per group is the investment playbook: the screening gates that define what a qualified deal looks like, the scoring rubrics calibrated to that domain's risk profile, and the SME pool who bring the right domain expertise. You're not rebuilding the factory for MedicalGroup or SpaceGroup — you're swapping out the playbook and plugging in the domain experts.

Commands — What to Type

Type these in the Cowork chat window. Claude follows the corresponding workflow and updates Dealum automatically.

🔄
/sync-pipeline
Also runs automatically at session start

Pulls the latest Tech-tagged deals from Dealum and displays a live pipeline dashboard — deal count by stage, stalled deals, and suggested next actions.

🔍
/screen [company]
Stage: Inbox → Screening

Runs the TechGroup triage screener: 3 hard pass/fail gates, then Opportunity scoring (/25) and Readiness scoring (/20). Returns a verdict (Advance / Watch / Kill) with a Triage Report saved to the deals/ folder.

Output: [Company] - Triage Report.docx
🤝
/scout [company]
Stage: Screening → Scout/IntroCall

Runs the Scout Q assessment (Phase 1 viability + Phase 2 depth questions). Maps the deal to a TechGroup theme. Outputs a one-sentence verdict, the single biggest risk, and 3–5 targeted diligence questions for the intro call.

Output: [Company] - Scout Assessment Report.docx
🔬
/diligence [company]
Stage: Scout → Diligence

Launches two research agents in parallel (company-researcher + competitive-intelligence), applies scoring rubrics (moat + risk), and generates the DD Kickoff Package with 17-folder assignments for the DD team.

Output: [Company] - DD Kickoff Package.docx
📋
/dd-report [company]
Stage: Diligence → DD Report

Run after the DD team completes the 17-folder work. Synthesizes all prior findings into 11 scored sections (1–5 scale, RAG-colored). This is the primary IC briefing document — a structured Word report, not a free-form memo.

Output: [Company]-NWAi-DD-Report.docx
✅
/decision [company] [invest|pass|watch]
Stage: DD Report → Decision

Records the IC decision (Invest / Pass / Watch) directly into Dealum and applies the appropriate tags. No document output — this is purely a pipeline and CRM action.

Output: Dealum updated
📝
/memo [company]
Stage: Decision → Memo (archival)

Generates a 4-slide PPTX Executive Summary in NWAi branding (navy/ice-blue). Primarily for archival use or external IC presentations. The DD Report is the working IC briefing; the memo is the polished final artifact.

Output: [Company]-NWAi-Exec-Summary.pptx

AI Agents — Claude's Research Team

Agents are autonomous subprocesses Claude launches to do legwork. They run in parallel, report back with a structured briefing, and feed their findings into the relevant command output.

📊 pipeline-monitor

Triggered by: session start, /sync-pipeline, "what's in the pipeline?"

Connects to Dealum, pulls all Tech-tagged deals, groups them by pipeline stage, and surfaces stalled deals with suggested next actions.

Returns: Live pipeline dashboard — deal counts per stage, attention items, recommended next command.

🕵️ company-researcher

Triggered by: /diligence, or explicit request

Web research on the founding team (LinkedIn, Crunchbase, news coverage), funding history, traction signals, and any public red flags on the company or founders.

Returns: Structured founder + company briefing, IC-ready, incorporated into the DD Kickoff Package.

🗺️ competitive-intelligence

Triggered by: /diligence, or explicit request

Maps direct competitors and incumbent threats, validates market sizing data, assesses timing signals, and evaluates the company's moat positioning relative to the competitive landscape.

Returns: Competitive landscape briefing with market size data — feeds directly into moat and market scoring.

Investment Framework — The Playbook

Seven reference documents Claude loads when running analysis. These encode NWAi's proprietary criteria so the same standards are applied consistently, regardless of how compelling a pitch sounds.

Reference File What It Contains Used At
gates-and-flags-techgroup.md TechGroup triage screener: 3 hard gates (instant Kill if failed) + Opportunity scoring /25 + Readiness scoring /20 + decision thresholds Screen
scout-questions.md Phase 1 viability questions (rapid structural assessment) + Phase 2 depth questions (venture potential + moat) Scout
diligence-scoring-rubrics.md General Moat score (0–6), AI Moat score (0–10), Risk score (1–10, where 1=lowest), Bear/Base/Bull financial model Diligence
dd-checklist.md 17-folder due diligence framework with team assignment slots and gate-critical folder designations Diligence
ai-moats-framework.md Three moat types for evaluating AI company defensibility — used to assess whether a product has durable competitive advantage Scout + DD Report + Memo
dd-report-format-reference.md Exact layout spec for the DD Report: 11 sections, 2-column table format, RAG score colors, content rules DD Report
memo-format-reference.md 4-slide PPTX structure, NWAi branding spec, ++/-- notation, "What is the Bet?" framing Memo

Two analytical lenses — always active, at every stage

⚡ Structural Discontinuity

Is this Big Idea riding a genuine, irreversible market shift — or incremental improvement dressed up as disruption? A real discontinuity changes the rules of the game. An incremental product just plays the existing game a little better.

🔒 Memory Lock-in

Does the product get smarter, stickier, or more embedded the more it's used? Proprietary data flywheels, deep workflow integration, and switching costs driven by accumulated context are the hallmarks of a durable AI moat.

DD Report scoring — 11 sections, 1–5 scale, RAG colored

Score 4–5  → Green
Strong signal, advance confidently
Score 3  → Amber
Conditional — specific concerns to resolve
Score 1–2  → Red
Serious concern — likely Kill condition

Non-Negotiable Investment Criteria

Any deal that fails any of these is a Pass. No exceptions — regardless of how compelling the pitch or team appears.

📄

Structure

Equity required. Priced round preferred (Seed–Series A). No SAFEs. Convertible debt acceptable. C-Corp only.

🇺🇸

Geography

US-based HQ and IP. Executive team must be US-based. No foreign-owned entities.

💰

Traction

Real revenue or signed paying customers. Research projects and demo labs do not qualify.

🚀

Stage

MVP complete with at least one successful beta completed. TRL 5+ (GAO scale).

🤝

Syndication

Credible lead investor required. Cap table must be clean — no structural red flags.

📈

Return Potential

10× in 5 years minimum. TAM must support a 20×–100× return path.
