# NWAi Due Diligence Checklist

## Layer Architecture

The NWAi DD framework has two layers. Both are produced by the `/diligence` command.

**Layer 2 — Hypothesis Confirmation Plan (AI-Derived)**
Organized by validation domain. The AI pipeline synthesizes agent research into conclusions for six groups: Market, Technical, Team, Commercial, Competitive, Financial. Each group contains a hypothesis (from Scout thesis), an AI-generated conclusion, a signal (🟢/🟡/🔴), and the biggest remaining uncertainty. This is the analytical core — it anchors DD Report section scores.

Layer 2 → DD Report score mapping:
| Layer 2 Validation Group | DD Report Sections It Anchors |
|---|---|
| Market Validation | Market Opportunity, Market Timing |
| Technical Validation | Product / Technology, Moat & Defensibility |
| Team Validation | Management Team |
| Commercial Validation | Traction & Revenue, GTM |
| Competitive Validation | Competitive Landscape, Moat & Defensibility |
| Financial Validation | Financial Model, Return Path |

**Layer 1 — 17-Folder Data Completeness Checklist (below)**
Organized by document/data type. Its job is: *did we collect everything we need?* Not the basis for scoring — confirms the evidence is in hand. Each folder assigned to one DD team member.

## Layer 1: 17-Folder DD Assignment Checklist

This checklist matches the Data Room Setup. Each folder should be assigned to one DD team
member. Approach: fill in key boxes until a deal is killed — even killed deals may re-appear.

Best practice: assign each folder to one team member for accountability and completeness.

---

## Folder 1: Cap Table & Term Sheet
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- What is the fully diluted current cap table? What will the post-money cap table look like assuming the proposed deal?
- Who are the major investors on the cap table? Who will be our partners?
- How much equity do management and founders have? Is it cash invested or sweat equity only?
- Are SAFEs or convertible notes present? What is their impact on the fully diluted cap table?
- Is there an adequate option pool for new hires and adjustments?
- Has a deal advisor reviewed the term sheet? Are there non-standard or concerning provisions?

---

## Folder 2: Company Financial Model & NWA Assessment
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- Show the financial model the company is presenting. Review key assumptions.
- Stress-test key assumptions: revenue timing, pricing, staffing costs, cash needed to next milestone.
- Build NWAi's own high-level model (Revenue, EBITDA, Cash) — NWAi will typically be more pessimistic.
- Base any valuation recommendation on NWAi's own model, not the company's.
- When is the next fundraise needed? How much dilution is expected?

---

## Folder 3: Company Pitch Deck(s) & Business Plan
**Assigned to:** _______________

Contents to gather:
- Copies of each version of the pitch deck
- Full business plan if available
- One-pager or executive summary

---

## Folder 4: Competition
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- Who has the company identified as competition? What are their stated strengths and weaknesses of competitors? Do we agree?
- Does the company view competition statically or dynamically? (Static analysis = red flag)
- Can we identify additional competitors via Google, LinkedIn, Crunchbase, patent searches, clinical trial databases?
- How does the company respond when presented with competitors they haven't mentioned?
- What risks exist that competitors are working on a similar solution and will get there first?
- What is the competitive moat? (Reference AI Moats Framework)

---

## Folder 5: Customer Problem Being Solved
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- How does the customer currently address this problem? How big an improvement does this product offer?
- Is this a major pain point (must-have) or nice-to-have?
- Does the price of the product reflect the value provided?
- Who within the customer organization needs to sign off on this purchase? (Decision-maker map)
- For two-sided marketplaces or complex sales (e.g., doctor + hospital + insurance): who must all be convinced?
- If creating a new category: why will customers pay for this new benefit? Does the unit economics work for the customer?

---

## Folder 6: Due Diligence Team Communications & Deliberations
**Assigned to:** DD Lead

Contents:
- All email traffic and documented deliberations among DD team
- Captures depth of team's analysis for the Investment Committee

---

## Folder 7: Executive Summary
**Assigned to:** DD Lead

Contents:
- Standard two-page summary of the deal for NWAi's Investment Committee
- Must note opportunities AND risks associated with the deal

---

## Folder 8: Exit Assessment & Risks
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- NWAi target: 10x return in 5 years. Based on post-money valuation and projected future rounds, why can this deal achieve 10x+?
- Are there early exit opportunities triggered by milestones (partnership, regulatory approval, customer count)?
- Who are the logical acquirers? Name specific companies.
- Is management incentivized to pursue a timely exit?
- What are the one or two biggest risks that could prevent success?

---

## Folder 9: Go-to-Market Plan
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- What is the GTM plan? Does it fit the industry's and customers' buying habits?
- Do they have data on CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) and LTV (Lifetime Value)? LTV/CAC > 3x?
- Are they adequately funded to achieve the traction they're forecasting?
- Do they understand the customer decision-making timeline and variables (e.g., long-term contracts with competitors)?
- Do the gross margins support the long-term economics of the sales strategy?
- Do they have clear metrics and leverage key opinion leaders?
- Are they overly dependent on one customer or one channel?
- Do they have adequate capital to fund the balance sheet to support sales growth?

---

## Folder 10: Intellectual Property (IP)
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- Are patents issued? How broad and defensible are they? Will they last long enough to contribute to a good exit?
- Is all IP owned by the company, or licensed? If licensed, what are all the terms and milestone payments?
- Was IP purchased? Are there any claims (especially if universities are involved)?
- If patents are infringed, can the company adequately defend them financially?
- If not patented: what other IP exists (trade secrets, proprietary data, algorithms)?
- Is the IP assigned to the company, or is any core technology held personally or by a prior employer?
- Does the IP provide a strong competitive moat?

---

## Folder 11: Timeline to Market / Milestones
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- What are the key milestones on the development and commercialization timeline?
- If regulatory approvals are required (FDA, FCC, etc.): have they allocated enough time and money?
- Do they have an adequate runway to reach market? If not, when is the next raise and how much dilution is expected?
- Overlay milestones and cash requirements on the timeline — do they match?

---

## Folder 12: Management Team, Board & Advisors
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- Is the CEO strong and coachable?
- Is there depth below the CEO? Who are the key players?
- What skills are critical for this company to succeed? Does the team have them?
- How experienced is the team? Is experience directly relevant to this industry?
- Are all team members fully committed (no day jobs)?
- Have background checks been run on key executives?
- Is the board independent? Does it have the skills needed to guide the company?
- Will NWAi have a board seat or observer rights?
- How much equity does management collectively hold?
- Are non-competes and NDAs in place for senior management?
- Any reason to think a prior employer would have a claim to a founder's work?
- Are advisors genuine KOLs or just CEO friends?
- Are there voting agreements or rights of first refusal in place?

---

## Folder 13: Manufacturing Plans & Product Status
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- Are there challenges moving from prototype/MVP to final production? (Common for hardware/deep tech)
- Does the company have a clear picture of the final manufactured product and its costs?
- Will they produce themselves or use contract manufacturing? If contract, who and why?
- Include all agreements with consultants, designers, or manufacturers.
- What is the Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRL)?

---

## Folder 14: Market Size, Segments & Target Customers
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- How big is the total addressable market (TAM) and the immediately serviceable market (SAM)?
- Are there multiple market segments? Does the product address all of them, or just a few?
- If entering multiple markets sequentially: how big are those markets, and is the plan adequately funded?
- Is the market growing or contracting? At what CAGR?
- How much innovation has occurred in the market in the past decade? Are incumbents discussing new innovations?
- How entrenched are incumbents? What will it take to steal market share?
- Is the timing right — or too early/too late?

---

## Folder 15: Historical Financials, Balance Sheet, Taxes & Other
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- Gather prior year(s) and YTD financials, including current balance sheet.
- Does the balance sheet have unusual debt? Is any debt owed to management or shareholders that isn't converting in this round?
- Review prior year(s) tax returns.
- Gather corporate documents (business licenses, incorporation documents, current Certificate of Insurance).
- Do historical financials support the traction claimed in the pitch deck? If not, why?

---

## Folder 16: Product Promise, Testing, Status & Next Steps
**Assigned to:** _______________

Questions to answer:
- What is the product promise? How has testing validated it?
- How much more testing is needed before the product is finalized?
- What is the current development status and timeline to market-ready?
- What are the key next steps?
- What is the TRL (Technology Readiness Level)? Must be ≥ 5 for NWAi.

---

## Folder 17: Recordings of Company Presentations
**Assigned to:** DD Lead

Contents:
- NWA records most company presentations
- Links/files to all relevant recordings
