# NWAi Executive Summary Memo — Format Reference

This document defines the structure, content, and formatting for the NWAi Investment
Executive Summary — the 4-slide PPTX deck generated by the `/memo` command. This is the
IC-ready presentation shared with NWA members before a vote.

Derived from the NWA standard format (reference: Coagulo Medical Technologies, September 2020).

---

## Output Format

- **File type:** .pptx (PowerPoint)
- **Slides:** 4
- **Filename:** `[Company-Name]-NWAi-Exec-Summary-[YYYY-MM-DD].pptx`
- **Branding:** "New World Angels — Confidential Investment Report" in header on slides 2–4
- **Color palette:** Navy (`1E2761`) primary, ice blue (`CADCFC`) accent, white text

---

## Slide 1 — Cover

**Purpose:** Title page. Establishes company, investment amount, and date.

**Content elements:**
- Title: "Executive Summary"
- Subtitle: "Supports NWA [Voluntary/Lead] $[Amount] Investment in [Company Full Legal Name]"
- Date: [Month DD, YYYY] — use the IC vote or members call date
- NWA logo placeholder (top or bottom)

**Design:** Dark background (navy). White text. Clean and minimal.

---

## Slide 2 — Deal Snapshot & Company Narrative

**Purpose:** All the facts a member needs to understand the deal structure and what the company does.

**Left column — Deal Metadata (structured data blocks):**

| Block | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Investment Timing | Key dates: Stage approval, refresher call, members call, funding close |
| Industry | Single line (e.g., "Medical Devices/Diagnostic", "Enterprise SaaS", "AI Infrastructure") |
| Due Diligence Team | Names of DD leads and team members |
| Capital Structure | Pre-money valuation, round type (e.g., Series A Preferred), liquidation preference, NWA check size range, total round size |
| Post-Round Cap Table | Table: Investor name → % ownership. Include NWA, lead VC, other investors, founders |

**Right column — Narrative Paragraphs (dense prose, ~3–5 sentences each):**

1. **Summary** — What the company does, who it serves, the core problem it solves, and why it matters now. Plain language.
2. **Technology / Intellectual Property** — What the technology is, what's proprietary, IP status (patents, licenses, exclusivity), TRL level, current prototype/product stage.
3. **Management Team** — Founder(s) names, titles, credentials, domain expertise, prior experience. Key hires. Board and advisor quality.
4. **Market & Competition** — Target market, TAM size (with source), key market dynamics, why now, and how the competitive landscape looks (name competitors or state "no direct competitors" with rationale).

## Slide 3 — Financial Model & Use of Funds

**Purpose:** Show where the money goes and what the financial upside looks like.

**Top section — Economic Model narrative (~2–4 sentences):**
- Describe the business/revenue model (e.g., razor/razor-blade, SaaS subscription, transactional)
- State the primary exit scenario and expected timeline (acquisition, IPO)
- Note gross margin profile if available
- Mention any existing strategic buyer conversations or LOIs

**Middle section — Use of Funds (itemized list):**
- List each budget line with dollar amount
- Show total round size at bottom
- Include any notes on non-dilutive funding (grants, SBIR, etc.) that offset line items

**Bottom section — Pro Forma Forecast (table):**

| $-MM | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 | Year 4 | Year 5 |
|------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Total Revenue | | | | | |
| EBITDA | | | | | |

Use company projections as baseline; note if NWAi model differs materially.

---

## Slide 4 — Analysis & Recommendation

**Purpose:** The verdict slide. NWA's own assessment — not the company's pitch.

**Analysis narrative (~2–4 sentences):**
A blunt summary of the investment thesis. What makes this company uniquely positioned? What is the core value driver?

**Strengths (using `++/--` notation):**
- `++` = clear strength
- `++/--` = strength with a significant caveat
- `-` = minor concern bundled with a strength
Use 4–6 bullet points. Be specific, not generic.

**Risks (using `--/-/+` notation):**
- `--` = significant risk
- `-` = moderate concern
- `-/+` = risk with a mitigant
Use 3–5 bullet points. Cover team, technology, market, regulatory, financial as applicable.

**Recommendation block:**
- Bold header: "Recommendation: [INVEST / WATCH / PASS] $[Amount]"
- 2–4 sentences: State the core bet, why NWA is the right investor, and what success looks like.

**"What is the Bet?" (single sentence, bold or callout box):**
A one-sentence thesis statement of the form:
> "[Company] will [exit path/outcome] at a [X]x+ multiple in [timeframe], [conditional on key milestone]."

Example: *"Coagulo will be acquired by a strategic competitor at a 5x+ multiple in the next 18–24 months, or alternatively will grow to become a large revenue-generating company in the coagulation diagnostics space."*

## Content Sourcing

When generating the memo, pull content from these sources in priority order:

1. **Dealum application** — `get_application(id)` for deal metadata, description, contact info
2. **Prior pipeline outputs** — Screening report (flags), Scout Assessment (theme, Phase 1/2 findings), Diligence package (DD checklist results, scoring rubrics)
3. **Web research** — Company website, Crunchbase, founder LinkedIn, recent press
4. **AI Moats Framework** — `ai-moats-framework.md` — apply to Technology/IP section and Strengths/Risks

Mark any missing field as `[TO BE CONFIRMED]` — do not fabricate data.

---

## Tone & Language Standards

- **Strengths/Risks:** Use NWA's `++/--` notation. Not generic bullet points.
- **"What is the Bet?":** One sentence. No hedging. IC-ready.
- **Narratives:** Dense prose. No sub-bullets inside narrative paragraphs.
- **Numbers:** Always include source or methodology for TAM/SAM figures.
- **Recommendation:** Lead with the verdict. Dollar amount. Then rationale.
